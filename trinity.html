The Trinity Profile is the foundational document of The Labyrinth. It maps you across three dimensions — Mind, Spirit, Soul — plus the fourth instrument most people have stopped listening to: the Body. Nothing in the school builds without it. The map comes first because you cannot navigate a labyrinth you have not yet agreed to enter.

Its accuracy depends entirely on what you are willing to name.

GATE I
Mind

How you actually think —
not how you believe you do.

Most people have a story about their own mind. They are a big picture thinker. They work best under pressure. They are creative but struggle with follow-through. These stories are not the map — they are the map the mind has built to protect itself from a more accurate one.

The Mind section does not ask you to identify yourself. It asks you to tell a story. What you built. What you started and didn't finish. A decision you made last year and how it actually happened. The cognitive architecture reveals itself in the telling — in what you reach for first, how many directions appear while you're answering, whether the energy peaks at the idea or at the completion.

What emerges is the actual operating sequence of your mind. The wiring beneath the story. The failure mode that has a specific shape. The gap between who you believe yourself to be and what the people closest to you see when you are under pressure.

GATE II
Spirit

The weight you carry
without knowing its name.

The Spirit section is the most defended territory. Everyone has narratives here — about their emotional health, their relationships, their capacity for love. The narratives are not the map. They are what we built to survive the gap between who we wanted to be and what actually happened.

What lives here: the grief that is still running. The cycle that repeats across every relationship regardless of who the other person is. The thing you do at your absolute worst, when you are exhausted and cornered, that you would rather not look at directly. What you reach for when you are carrying more than you can hold and there is no good option available.

The shadow does not yield to direct questions. It reveals itself through what you defend, through what others have kept bumping into, through the story you tell about the person you have hurt most. Iris comes at it from the back — because that is the only door that opens.

GATE III
Soul

What was there
before the world touched you.

The Soul section goes somewhere older than memory and older than personality. It asks for a specific childhood memory — before school told you what to be good at, before anyone had opinions about who you were. What were you doing with your mind? What were you endlessly drawn to? What did you notice that other children didn't seem to notice?

This is the pre-dispositional layer. The encoded architecture that was present before experience formed character. The numerological chart calculated from the name on your birth certificate. The astrological signature of the moment you arrived. These are not decorative — they are the coordinates of the soul, read from the systems that have been tracking this data for thousands of years.

What emerges from this section is the mission beneath the biography. Not a career. Not a role. Something that would be lost if you weren't here to pass it forward — and the honest distance between that transmission and what you are actually doing with your days right now.

"The mirror of true self-reflection lies atop the pillar of adversity."

GATE IV
Body

The fourth instrument —
and the most honest.

The body is the oldest intelligence in the room. It has been signaling things the mind has been too busy to hear. It knows where the nervous system is dysregulated. It knows what it has been carrying that hasn't been resolved. It knows — most of the time — exactly what the solution looks like. It has been pointing toward it for longer than you've been building frameworks about why the body might be right.

Most people who arrive at the fracture point have already identified the answer their body is pointing toward. They have a sense of the place, the way of living, the environment that would let them breathe. The Body section is where that gets named explicitly rather than remaining as a feeling that hasn't been spoken aloud.

What the body knows becomes part of the profile. The mineral deficiencies. The grounding practices. The frequency and resonance architecture. The named solution — stated directly as its own section, because the body's prescription deserves to be heard as clearly as the mind's analysis.

What You Receive

A document that belongs to you entirely.

At the end of the interview, Iris assembles a complete Trinity Profile — a multi-page document that synthesizes everything surfaced across all four dimensions. Your numerological chart calculated from your exact legal name. Your astrological signature with all seven placements interpreted in combination, not isolation. Your 28-day biological cycle tracked to the current date. Your cognitive architecture mapped from the stories you told rather than the descriptions you offered. Your shadow work named with precision — the pattern, its cost, who it touches.

The document is yours to download immediately. It is not stored, sold, or used for any other purpose. It is the map. The work of The Labyrinth begins from there.

How this works

This is a structured discovery conversation, not a quiz or form. Iris will ask you questions and follow threads. Your words are captured verbatim to build the profile — nothing is softened or summarized. The profile is only as accurate as what you bring in.

Nothing shared is stored server-side or used for any purpose other than producing your document. The interview runs entirely in your browser session. When you close it, the session is gone. The profile you download is the only record.

Set aside ninety minutes. Find a quiet place. Voice and text both work — speak or type, whichever feels more natural. The interview is built for voice.
